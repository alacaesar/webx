Fur fashion to fight the fur industry

Every year, one hundred million animals are bred and killed to supply the fashion industry. That's why the International Anti-fur coalition launches UNFUR — a digital fur fashion collection. The complete earnings of the auction will be used to fight the fur industry worldwide.

The five fashion pieces are designed by amazing students at the University of Westminster and digitalized by Hexaware. As we are aware of the NFT's emissions, our collection is minted on the Flow blockchain. Flow blockchain requires just 0.18 gigawatt hours (GWh) of energy.

FLOW is a fast, decentralized, and developer-friendly blockchain, designed as the foundation for a new generation of games, apps, and the digital assets that power them. It is based on a unique, multi-role architecture, and designed to scale without sharding, allowing for massive improvements in speed and throughput while preserving a developer-friendly, ACID-compliant environment.

The amazingly talented students of the University of Westminster are at the heart of our collection. These second-year students have sacrificed their precious evening hours to bring the collection to life.

The University of Westminster boasts a rich history and has been providing students with academic excellence, cultural engagement and personal enrichment. The Fashion Design BA has an exceptional reputation for being aligned with the international fashion industry.

Behind every piece of fur

is a heart that used to beat

The complete earnings of the UNFUR collection auction will be used by the International Anti-Fur Coalition to fight the fur industry. After the mint, the IAFC will also receive the rightful share for the future resell of the different NFTs.

The International Anti-Fur Coalition (IAFC) is an Animal Rights (AR) movement focusing on the atrocities of the fur trade. It was founded in 2006. The coalition consists of over 50 Anti-Fur organizations worldwide, that are working together to bring an end to the horror of the fur industry.

We organize simultaneous “Global days of Action"; e.g.: Demonstrations, Marches, “Information days” and special events are held regularly all over the world.
